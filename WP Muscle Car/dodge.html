Dodge


The founders of Dodge are two brothers, John and Horace Dodge, born in Niles, Michigan. At a young age, they already had a reputation for quality and excellence in the automotive industry. In 1902, Henry Ford offered the brothers 10% of his company in return for automotive parts for his Ford vehicles. Shortly after, in 1910, the new factory in Michigan quickly became one of the largest automotive parts companies in the world. The Dodges had a big part of the all-American first car, the Model-T. They made the engines, transmissions, and other parts for this legendary car.


After a decade of supplying parts for Ford, the Dodge brothers decided to resign and refused to work with Ford. Whatever their reason might have been, the Dodge brothers still valued quality and durability. So on July 17, 1914, the Dodge brotherâ€™s decision to begin selling their vehicles and the Dodge Brothers Company began. A few months later, their very first vehicle, the four-cylinder Dodge Model, was born. With their excellent reputation, the brothers had no problem and sold well. The Dodge brand quickly made its foundation on dependability.


Dodge Charger R/T 1970


The 1970 Dodge Charger R/T was one of the performance leaders for the line that year. This model was available in either a six-cylinder or a popular V-8 engine.
The 1970 Dodge models were designed under the direction of Elwood P. Engel, vice president of design, and led the way in muscle car sales during the decade. The 1970 Dodge Charger models carried the same body style introduced in 1968-1969, and many younger drivers and car enthusiasts enjoyed its great styling.
Dodge positioned the Charger as a premium mid-sized car. You will find a striking resemblance between the first-generation Dodge Charger and Dodge Coronet. Moreover, the Dodge Charger turned out to be amongst the top muscle cars.
The beautiful vehicle harboured faux wood-grain steering wheel, premium trim, vinyl upholstery, and bucket seats. You should also know that it had V8 engines with carrying displacements. You can also find three-speed automatic and column-mounted three-speed manual transmission models.
